Case 74

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
A 51-year-old married white female is referred by her cardiologist for evaluation of a left adrenal mass.  One month ago she had computerized tomography of the chest for further evaluation of an abnormal chest x-ray.  The CT showed a small left pleural effusion, slight atelectasis of the left lung base posteriorly and a 2.5 x 2.0 cm left adrenal mass.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY
She has a history of hypertension, cardiac arrhythmia, pneumonia, motor vehicle accident with head and chest injuries, repair of cerebrospinal fluid leak, empty sella, hysterectomy and a hiatal hernia with gastroesophageal reflux two-three months ago.

SOCIAL HISTORY
She has smoked 1 pack of cigarettes daily for approximately 30 years.

MEDICATIONS
Medications include furosemide, potassium, metoprolol, verapamil and lorazepam.  Family history is positive for cancer, heart disease, hypertension, strokes and diabetes mellitus.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Physical examination shows an obese female with a height of 167.6 cm and weight of 90.0 kg.  Her obesity involves extremities and torso.  Blood pressure is 130/88 mmHg.  Pulse is 72/min and regular.  She has bilateral thickened tympanic membranes and crackles at both lung bases.  The remainder of the examination is normal.  She does not have hirsutism or striae.

LABORATORY AND OTHER PROCEDURES
Complete blood count shows a hematocrit of 49.5% and WBC of 10,000/mm3 with a normal differential.  Chemistry profile shows the following:  sodium-138 mEq/L, potassium-4.3 mEq/L, chloride-101 mEq/L, CO2-28 mEq/L, BUN-16 mg/dL, creatinine-1.0 mg/dL, cholesterol-234 mg/dL, triglycerides-354 mg/dL. calcium-9.5 mg/dL, phosphorous-4.2 mg/dL, total protein-7.1 g/dL, albumin-4.5 g/dL, uric acid-5.4 mg/dL, glucose-82 mg/dL.  Plasma cortisol at 0800=11 micrograms/dL.
