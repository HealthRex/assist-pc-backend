Case 76

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
A 19-year-old single female is referred for evaluation of "spells".  Over the past 6 months she has had 7 "spells" characterized by sudden onset of weakness, sweating, light headedness, palpitations and tremor.  The episodes usually occur in the mid morning or mid afternoon.  Her symptoms are usually relieved within 10 minutes by eating crackers and drinking a glass of orange juice.  Because the symptoms seemed to be more intense during the last episode 9 days ago she decided to seek medical attention.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY
Her past medical history is unremarkable except for tonsillectomy at age 3.

MEDICATIONS
She is taking no medications.

FAMILY HISTORY
Family history is positive for hypertension, strokes, and adult onset type diabetes mellitus.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Physical examination reveals a thin white female with a height of 160 cm and weight of 44.1 kg.  She has moderate pectus excavatum, a grade II/VI systolic murmur loudest over the base and hyperactive deep tendon reflexes.  The remainder of the  physical examination is normal.

LABORATORY AND OTHER PROCEDURES
Complete blood count shows a hematocrit of 36%, normal red blood cell indices and WBC of 4100/mm3 with a normal differential.  Urinalysis shows a specific gravity of 1.025, ph 6.0, 2-5 WBC/high powered field and no glucose or protein.  Chemistry profile results are as follows:  sodium-142 mEq/L; potassium-3.8 mEq/L; chloride-107 mEq/L; CO2-26 mEq/L; BUN-8.0 mg/dL; creatinine-0.7 mg/dL; fasting blood glucose-82 mg/dL; calcium-9.1 mg/dL; phosphorous-4.7 mg/dL; cholesterol-144 mg/dL and triglycerides-54 mg/dL.
