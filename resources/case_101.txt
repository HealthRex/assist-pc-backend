Case 101

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
A 24-year-old woman was admitted to the surgical service two days ago and underwent an uncomplicated appendectomy for acute appendicitis.  In response to a detailed review of systems, the patient denies polyuria, kidney stones, peptic ulcer disease, depression, constipation or musculoskeletal complaints.  She jogs five miles daily.  Her last menstrual period ended ten days ago, and she has never been pregnant.

MEDICATIONS
The patient takes birth control pills, but denies other prescription or over-the-counter medications, including vitamins.  Her typical diet is normal with an estimated calcium intake of 900-1000 mg daily.

OCCUPATIONAL HISTORY
She works as an investment banker.

SOCIAL HISTORY
She does not smoke cigarettes, denies illicit drugs, and drinks occasional white wine.

FAMILY HISTORY
Her mother had a parathyroidectomy eight years ago after hypercalcemia was detected during hospitalization for cholecystitis.  There is no other significant family history.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Physical examination is normal except for a recent surgical wound in the right lower quadrant.  Blood pressure is 110/60 mmHg and pulse rate is 70/min.

LABORATORY AND OTHER PROCEDURES
Hemoglobin 14.0 g/dL, hematocrit 43%, WBC 5400/mm3 and platelets 245,000/mm3.  Urine specific gravity 1.020, pH 6, negative for glucose, blood and protein with no RBC and 0-3 WBC/HPF.  Glucose 95 mg/dL, BUN 10 mg/dL, creatinine 0.6 mg/dL, sodium 140 mEq/L, potassium 4.3 mEq/L, chloride 105 mEq/L, bicarbonate 25 mEq/L, calcium 11.5 mg/dL, phosphate 4.0 mg/dL, total protein 7.0 g/dL, alkaline phosphate is normal, albumin 4.0 g/dL, and magnesium 2.4 mEq/L.  Chest x-ray, electrocardiogram and hand films are normal.  Free thyroxine 2.0 ng/dl (0.8-2.4), free tri-iodothyronine 500 pg/dl (230-660), 25-hydroxy vitamin D3 40 ng/ml (15-80), and parathyroid hormone 60 pg/ml (13-64).
