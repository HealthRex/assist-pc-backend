Case 69

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
A 37-year-old white female is referred by her family physician for thyroid evaluation.
She has complained of trembling spells associated with feeling hot and flushed for 3 months.
She has noted tiredness and lack of energy for 4 months.
Her weight has been stable during this time.
Chronic constipation has been worse recently.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY
There is no history of a goiter.
She has a history of chronic constipation.
Past medical history indicates a previous hysterectomy and cholecystectomy 11 years ago,
hereditary angioedema and a recent history of hypercholesterolemia.

MEDICATIONS
Her medications include 400 mg ibuprofen 3 times daily, 0.25 mg digoxin daily,
stool softener twice daily and 2 mg stanozolol daily for angioedema.

FAMILY HISTORY
There is no family history of thyroid disease.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Physical examination shows an obese white female who is 160 cm tall and weighs 87.7 kg.
Blood pressure is 148/90 mmHg.
Pulse is 84/min and regular.
She has increased hair on her face, abdomen, arms and lower back.
The remainder of the physical examination is normal.
She does not have a goiter and her deep tendon reflexes are normal.

LABORATORY AND OTHER PROCEDURES
Laboratory results recently obtained by her physician included the following:
CBC showed HCT 39%; WBC 6700/mm3 with a normal differential.
Urinalysis showed a specific gravity of 1.005, pH 6.0, negative for glucose, protein and blood.
Chemistry profile results were as follows: Albumin 4.3 g/dL; globulins 2.4 g/dL, Ca 9.3 mg/dL; Phosphate 3.2 mmHg;
bilirubin 0.3 mg/dL; alkaline phosphatase 90 IU/L; SGOT 33 U/L; SGPT 52 U/L; Na 143 mEq/L; K 3.9 mEq/L;
Cl 107 mEq/L;C02 24 mEq/L; BUN 12 mg/dL; Creatinine 1.0 mg/dL; Cholesterol 187 mg/dL;
triglycerides 312 mg/dL and glucose 117 mg/dL.
Serum thyroxine-↓3.1 μg/dL, T3 uptake-↑56%, free thyroxine index-1.7, serum triiodothyronine- 67 ng/dL, TSH 0.62 μIU/mL.
