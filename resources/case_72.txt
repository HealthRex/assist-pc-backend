Case 72

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
A 54-year-old Black man presented one month ago because of high blood pressure noted during a health screen done by his employer.  At that time his blood pressure was 164/102 mmHg.  His previous health exam was more than three years ago, and he was not informed of any medical problems.  He has returned now for follow-up.  Systems review is unremarkable.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY
There is no significant past medical history.

FAMILY HISTORY
His mother is alive at 78 and has diabetes mellitus; his father died of a stroke at age 67.  The patient has two brothers who are healthy and one sister who had high blood pressure during a pregnancy.

MEDICATIONS
The patient denies taking any medications or illicit drugs.

Heart size is normal with a grade II/VI systolic ejection murmur and an S4 present.  No bruits or edema.  The remainder of the exam is unremarkable.
